With the utterance of bahala na, one can be said to somehow rely a little part of their effort on forces outside himself, consequently hoping for a positive outcome (Fonseca et aI., 2005). Bahala na brings with it the belief that "one is not hopeless" (Jocano, 1999,p. 116)and that he can do something to alleviate hissituation.

. Perhaps in uttering bahala na, a person intuitively accesses his resources and starts hoping for positive results while the vision of the goal sustains him throughout the waiting process. Taken further in this light, Jocano (1999) believes that the Filipino hopes when uttering bahala na because he knows he can do something to attain his objective even when the situation is glaringly difficult and unfamiliar.

Uttering bahala na is coupled with an implicit trust in one's capacity to face the future situation
